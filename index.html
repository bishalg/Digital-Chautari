A New Paradigm for Public Engagement

Moving beyond traditional feedback mechanisms, Digital Chautari addresses the core challenges of modern governance with a foundation of cryptographic trust.

What is Digital Chautari?

It is a secure, decentralized protocol for public consultation and voting. It's an open standard, not a single app, that allows anyone to build tools for democratic engagement on a trustworthy, auditable foundation.

Why is it Needed?

To bridge the trust gap between citizens and government. Traditional surveys are opaque and vulnerable to bias. Digital Chautari provides mathematical proof that every voice is counted correctly and that results are tamper-proof, fostering public confidence.

How Does It Work?

Using cutting-edge cryptography. Citizens verify their identity once to get a private digital credential. They can then vote anonymously. All votes are encrypted and publicly tallied, allowing anyone to audit the final count for fairness.

The Lifecycle of a Verifiable Vote

From a citizen's voice to a transparent result, every step is secured by cryptography, ensuring privacy and integrity.

1

Identity Verification

A citizen securely verifies their National ID once with a trusted authority to receive a private, reusable `CitizenAttributeCredential` on their device.

2

Anonymous Voting

When a poll is available, the citizen uses their credential to cast a vote. A Zero-Knowledge Proof confirms their eligibility without revealing their identity.

3

Encrypted & Public

The anonymous vote is encrypted and posted to a public, transparent bulletin board. Individual votes cannot be read, but their existence is public record.

4

Verifiable Tally

All encrypted votes are mathematically combined into a final tally. Anyone can independently run the same calculation to verify the final result is 100% accurate and tamper-free.

Concept Note: Digital Chautari

To: Nepali Government Officials and Policymakers

From: Proponents of Digital Governance

Date: September 7, 2025

Subject: Digital Chautari - A Secure Digital Bridge Between Citizens and Government

1. The Problem: A Growing Trust Deficit

In the digital age, the channels between the government and its citizens remain largely traditional, slow, and opaque. Public consultations often fail to capture a representative sample of public opinion, and citizens lack confidence that their voices are heard or that official decisions are free from bias. This leads to a trust deficit that hampers effective governance and national development.

2. The Vision: A Tool for Transparent Governance

We propose the **Digital Chautari (“Digital Public Square”) Initiative**, a project to develop a secure, transparent, and verifiable digital toolkit for public consultation. This system is not just another survey app; it is a foundational piece of public infrastructure designed to provide policymakers with an un-tamperable, clear, and direct line to the true voice of the Nepali people. For the first time, from a constitutional referendum to a local ward’s budget priorities, we can collect public will with mathematical certainty.

3. How It Works: Security and Anonymity by Design
  • **One Person, One Vote:** Citizens verify their identity once using their National ID in a secure, private process. This grants them a digital credential that allows them to participate in any poll or vote they are eligible for.
  • **Complete Anonymity:** When a citizen voices their opinion, their identity is cryptographically separated from their vote. No one—not even the government—can know how an individual voted.
  • **End-to-End Verifiability:** Every vote is encrypted and posted to a public, transparent "digital bulletin board." The final tally is computed from these encrypted votes, and anyone can independently verify that the result is 100% accurate and has not been manipulated.
4. Benefits for Nepal
  • **Enhanced Public Trust:** Rebuilds faith in democratic processes by making them transparent and auditable.
  • **Data-Driven Policy:** Provides policymakers with accurate, unbiased data on public sentiment, leading to more effective and responsive governance.
  • **Inclusive Participation:** Gives a voice to minorities and those in remote areas, ensuring that policies reflect the needs of all Nepalis.
  • **Layered Governance:** The system is designed for Nepal’s federal structure, enabling polls at the national, provincial, and local municipal/ward levels.
5. The Path Forward

This is not a proposal to replace existing democratic institutions but to strengthen them with modern, secure technology. We have prepared a comprehensive white paper and technical specification detailing the protocol. We request a meeting to present this vision and discuss a pilot project, demonstrating how Digital Chautari can become a cornerstone of the nation’s digital future.
